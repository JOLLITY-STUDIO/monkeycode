Awhile back I compiled the script for the original Warsong and posted
it here. Some time afterwards, someone emailed me saying how it would
be cool if I could the same thing for Langrisser(Warsong) 2 as well,
seeing how both the ROM and the translation patch are somewhat hard
to find. I asked one of the people who did the translating about it, and 
he not only gave approval, he also sent me a text file containing almost 
all the English text in the scenarios! So even though my name is
on this file, it's really due to the work all the people on the Hiryuu
Honyaku translation team... and Masaya, of course (See end of credits
section for full details). I'm just the one who put it all together. In
other words, don't thank me, thank them. Flaming me is fine though ;).

The script has been kept the same as what's in the translation patch
except where there's an obvious typo or grammatical error, or where
the names become inconsistent. I've marked any place that I've made
changes to with a * in case anyone's curious. I've inserted text to say
when any given string of dialogue occurs, and also to give voice to the
occasional rant. They're all opinions, so feel free to be offended. It
won't mean anything (unless you're CJayC).

Secondly, this script was written for people who for some reason were
unable to get ahold of the translation patch and who still want to know
what's going on in the game. It is by no means perfect, and probably
has many things omitted. It was never meant to be a 100% comprehensive
look at the game, only to provide enough detail for someone to enjoy.
So while I've tried to get all the critical lines in, when it comes 
down to things like whether a character says "Yes, sir" or Roger,"
(which changes depending on who's alive and who's not) I get lazy.

Finally, this game contains a good deal of profanity. Most of you won't
care, but for those who do, be forewarned.

Hiring/Equipping Menu

This is where you hire troops and buy items for the upcoming battle.
The upper left shows a list of all your commanders. The upper right
shows the currently selected commander's stats, and the lower left
shows all of that commander's mercenaries. The menu itself shows up in
the lower right.

(From top to bottom)
> Hire troops > Select Commander > Select troop
> Inventory Equip > Select Commander > Equip Weapon > Equip Armor >
  Equip Accessory
> Shop > Buy
       > Sell
> General Post > (Takes you to General Post menu)

General Post

This is where you decide each hero's starting position. You'll be shown
all enemy and allied starting units, but things can change as a
scenario progresses.

> General Post > Select General > Select Position
> Auto Post > (Places your heroes for you)
> Survey > Select any allied (Green) or enemy (Red) commander >
  Shows information about that commander's unit.
> Attack > (Starts scenario)

Hero Command Menu:

This comes up whenever you select one of your characters.

> Move > Select move position > Confirm move >
   (If move brought you close enough to attack, do attack select)
> Attack > Select target for attack
> Magic > Select Spell > Select target > Confirm target
> Health > (Recovers 3 HP and 2 MP)
> Orders > Attack
         > Battle
         > Defend
         > Manual
                  > Sets the AI for that hero's mercenaries.

Options Menu: 
 
This comes up when you press the start button while in a scenario.

> Save > Save OK? (Yes/no) >
        (Saves to the mid-scenario save slot)
> Load > Choose game to load 
> Winning Condition > (Displays win/loss conditions) 
> Option > Game > (Choose between Fast and Normal)
         > Text > (Choose between Fast and Normal)
         > Battle Scene > (Choose between on and off)
         > Config > R
                  > G
                  > B > (Sets up the color of the window. Red, Green,
                         blue, you get the idea.)

                  > Controls > 
                        (Change the buttom for the selected command)
                      > Unit Cycle    > Confirm
                      > Cancel        > Menu
                  > Exit

A few questions/comments before I finally shut up. I've left them to
the end for the sake of those trying to just enjoy the ending. In fact,
if you want to enjoy the ending, I suggest you ignore the rambling of a
bored guy who enjoys chucking his ideas at others.

(The power of light is said, among other things, to be 'belief.'
 The Imperials believed in their fight at least as much as Elwin and
 company did. Their stands within the last few scenarios very similar
 to the last stands traditionally given to heroes. They've been no
 less heroic than those of the 'light'.)
(Elwin says at the end almost exactly what Bernhart said at the
 beginning. They seem to have given up on that argument.)
(Is Leon's assuming of Bernhart's ideals as his own the weakness 
 Bernhart was talking about? And for that matter, was Leon listening 
 to that conversation?)
(Both Egbert and Bernhart used the power of darkness, and they
 retained control of their wills till the very end. Egbert's 
 proposition to Jessica remains unanswered. Yet Elwin confidently
 states that darkness is unfit for humans.)
(What, might I ask, is not forged from people's desires?) 

The ending cutscences and character endings remain untranslated. I'd
be curious if anything interesting goes on in them. Evil seems to get
the best and final word on good, much like in Warsong... though
Garett's/Ledan's justice rebuttal makes more sense to me than anything
Elwin says in the final moments of the scenario.
